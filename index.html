Interests

Concurrency and Parallelism

The idea of being able to perform a task more efficiently by somehow dividing up the workload has always held a strong appeal for me, and since taking my first steps into implementing concurrency, it's become one of my favorite ways to improve runtime. Implementing it often proves difficult on multiple levels, providing the kind of challenges that I excel at. I also appreciate that it presents opportunities for making different design decisions, from leaning into the massively parallelized architecture of a GPU, with its unique strengths and weaknesses, to rebuilding a data-processing function from the ground up to stream individual objects so they can be handled by different cores or processors.

Algorithm Optimization

Just like concurrency and parallelism, algorithm optimization provides the ability to make a program run more efficiently, and that's why I enjoy it. Perhaps there's a bottleneck that could be removed, or maybe a program makes unnecessary passes over a data set. When dealing with algorithms, there are infinite possibilities that can be explored, particularly as complexity increases, so problems can stay varied and feel fresh even after encountering hundreds of them.

Big Data

I like the idea of Big Data for one reason: Any improvement made is magnified astronomically by the scale of the task. Thus, even mathematically small tweaks to efficiency or runtime can become worth chasing down thanks to their effect being upscaled to become practically significant. That means more opportunities for interesting problems to solve and discoveries to be made!
